The month is May and I have reached the last few weeks of BCIT CST Term 1 program.

A project that I am particularly proud of is Revival, a web-based application that will help solve problems related to extreme weather events. The aim behind the project was to design an app that will assist individuals affected by natural disasters and extreme weather conditions by providing critical updates and an interactive platform for local communities. Its purpose was to connect community members during post-impact relief efforts and facilitate the recovery process.

We approached the project through the following five categories: prevention, prediction, preparation, coping, and recovery. We designed the app so then after extreme weather events, information will be collected, and communities will be able to stay informed and recover. Through this project, I was able to learn to debug and problem-solve when portions of our code didn‚Äôt work. I was able to learn that programming involves a lot of problem-solving and it is important to be patient when things do not go as planned. I approached problems by (1) understanding the problem (2) devising a plan (3) carrying out the plan (4) looping back and reviewing the progress.
